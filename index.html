The Princess and the Pixel

Once upon a time, in the Kingdom of Bandwidth, there lived a princess so beautiful that even the dial-up modem would try to load her portrait — then give up after 47 minutes with a connection error.

She had been cursed by the Evil Wizard of 56k, who made her speak only in .wav files. The kingdom's sound card drivers hadn't been updated since 1997, so nobody could hear her.

Many brave knights rode in to rescue her by downloading her audio files, but the connection always dropped at 98%.

One day, a prince arrived bearing a T1 line. He downloaded her files in three seconds, heard her voice, fell deeply in love, and promptly installed the correct audio drivers.

The curse was broken. They were married in a ceremony streamed live at 240p. The buffering was rough during the vows, but everyone agreed it was beautiful.

~ The End ~

Jack and the Giant Download

Jack was a poor boy who traded his family's only computer for three magic floppy disks. His mother was furious and threw the disks out the window into the yard.

By morning, an enormous download progress bar had grown from the yard up through the clouds. Jack climbed it and discovered a castle belonging to a terrible giant who owned a magical goose that laid golden .exe files.

"FEE FI FO FUM!" bellowed the giant, "I SMELL THE IP ADDRESS OF AN ENGLISHMAN! YOUR MAC ADDRESS HAS BEEN LOGGED."

Jack snatched the golden .exe, slid back down the progress bar, and double-clicked it before reading the terms of service. The goose turned out to be adware — extremely profitable adware — and Jack's mother eventually forgave him.

The giant tried to sue for copyright infringement, but his lawyer communicated only via AIM and was entirely unreachable.

~ The End ~

Goldilocks and the Three Firewalls

Goldilocks was browsing one afternoon when she stumbled upon a server farm belonging to three bears: Papa Bear's Enterprise Firewall, Mama Bear's Corporate Proxy, and Baby Bear's Home Router — password still set to "password."

"Too restrictive," she said of Papa Bear's. "It won't even allow Wikipedia."

"Too slow," she said of Mama Bear's. "Everything routes through Cleveland."

"Just right," she said of Baby Bear's. She was inside in seconds.

She browsed, updated the router firmware, changed the admin password to something with 14 characters and two symbols, and left a strongly-worded sticky note about cybersecurity hygiene.

When the bears returned, they found their network significantly more secure. Their feelings were still hurt, so they filed a complaint with the HOA. The HOA's website was also running default credentials.

~ The End ~
